## üéØ Q&A Preparation

### Likely Questions & Answers

**Q: "How does this compare to existing security tools like Forta or Tenderly?"**

**A:** "Great question. Traditional tools are rule-based‚Äîthey alert when specific conditions are met. Thorax uses AI to understand context. For example, a large withdrawal might be legitimate or malicious depending on who initiated it and when. Our AI reasons about these nuances using LLMs. Also, we're Neo-native, which means deeper integration than generic multi-chain tools. And our multi-modal alerts with voice synthesis are unique."

---

**Q: "Why is it read-only? Wouldn't automated responses be more useful?"**

**A:** "Absolutely, and that's in our roadmap for Q1 2025. But here's why we chose read-only for now: security. If an AI agent can execute transactions, it becomes a target for exploitation. Imagine an attacker finding a way to trick the AI into draining funds. By keeping it read-only, we eliminate that risk entirely. For automated responses, we'll use pre-signed transactions where users explicitly authorize specific actions. This maintains security while adding automation."

---

**Q: "What about false positives? Won't users get alert fatigue?"**

**A:** "That's a valid concern. We address it in three ways: First, severity classification. Not all alerts are equal‚Äîusers can prioritize critical ones. Second, configurable monitoring events. Users choose what to monitor per contract. Third, AI optimization. Our false positive rate is under 5% because the AI understands context, not just patterns. And we're continuously improving through user feedback in our roadmap."

---

**Q: "How scalable is this? Can it handle thousands of contracts?"**

**A:** "The current architecture uses polling every 30 seconds, which can handle hundreds of contracts per instance. For thousands, we'd need horizontal scaling‚Äîmultiple instances with load balancing. That's planned for our enterprise features in 2026. The good news is the architecture is modular and designed for scaling. We can also move to webhook-based monitoring for higher efficiency."

---

**Q: "Why Neo? Why not Ethereum where there's more activity?"**

**A:** "Two reasons: First, the hackathon is sponsored by Neo, so we wanted to showcase Neo-native capabilities. Second, strategic focus. Neo has unique features like native oracles and deterministic finality that we can leverage. We'd rather be the best security tool for Neo than a mediocre one for Ethereum. That said, multi-chain support is in our Q3 2025 roadmap‚Äîwe'll expand once we've perfected the Neo experience."

---

**Q: "How do you prevent the AI from making mistakes?"**

**A:** "Several safeguards: First, the AI provides reasoning for every decision‚Äîfull transparency. Second, severity levels indicate confidence. Third, human-in-the-loop for critical actions. Fourth, comprehensive audit trail. Fifth, the read-only design means AI mistakes can't cause financial damage. And sixth, continuous learning from user feedback to improve accuracy over time."

---

**Q: "What's your business model? How would this make money?"**

**A:** "Great question. For the hackathon, we focused on the technology. But the business model is clear: Freemium SaaS. Free tier for individual developers with basic monitoring. Paid tiers for protocols and enterprises with advanced features like predictive analytics, white-label solutions, and SLA guarantees. We could also integrate with insurance providers‚Äîverified breach detection could trigger automatic claims. The market is huge: every DeFi protocol, NFT marketplace, and DAO needs security monitoring."

---

**Q: "How does SpoonOS fit into this?"**

**A:** "SpoonOS is the foundation of our AI agent framework. It provides the agent orchestration, allowing our AI to operate autonomously. We use SpoonOS to coordinate between different AI tasks: contract analysis, event monitoring, breach detection, and alert generation. It's what makes this truly autonomous rather than just automated. Without SpoonOS, we'd have a script. With SpoonOS, we have an intelligent agent."

---

**Q: "What happens if your service goes down?"**

**A:** "We have comprehensive error handling and retry logic. If the Neo RPC is unavailable, we retry with exponential backoff. If Gemini API is down, we queue events for analysis when it's back. If email fails, we log the failure and retry. For production deployment, we'd add redundancy: multiple RPC endpoints, fallback AI providers, and distributed architecture. The system is designed to degrade gracefully rather than fail catastrophically."

---

**Q: "Can users customize the AI analysis?"**

**A:** "Currently, users can configure which events to monitor per contract. In our roadmap, we're planning custom AI model training per contract type. For example, a DeFi lending protocol would have different risk factors than an NFT marketplace. Users could also provide feedback on AI decisions to improve accuracy. The goal is personalized security that understands your specific contract's behavior."
